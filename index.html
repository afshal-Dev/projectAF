Three Religious Festivals

Islam

Islamic festivals spread peace, gratitude, and unity among people. Muslims celebrate events like Ramadan and Eid with prayers and joy These occasions remind everyone to share love, help others, and thank Allah

christianity

Christian festivals are filled with love, hope, and togetherness. Celebrations like Christmas and Easter spread joy and peace around the world These festivals remind people of kindness, faith, and helping those in need

Hinduism

Hinduism is one of the oldest religions in the world, based on peace, karma, and devotion.It teaches belief in many gods and the cycle of birth and rebirth.Major Hindu festivals include Diwali, Holi, and Navratri, celebrated with joy and tradition

Islamic Festivals

Eid ul Fitr

Eid ul-Fitr is a joyous Islamic festival celebrated at the end of Ramadan, the month of fasting. Muslims gather for special prayers at mosques and open spaces. It is a time of forgiveness, charity, and community..

Eid ul Adha

Eid ul-Adha, also known as the Festival of Sacrifice, commemorates Prophet Ibrahim‚Äôs willingness to sacrifice his son in obedience to God. Muslims around the world perform the ritual of Qurbani, sacrificing an animal and distributing meat to the needy.

Muhrram

Muharram is the first month of the Islamic calendar and a period of reflection and mourning. The 10th day, known as Ashura, commemorates the martyrdom of Imam Hussain at Karbala

Christianity festival

Good Friday

Good Friday is a significant Christian festival that commemorates the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It is observed during Holy Week, two days before Easter Sunday. On this day, Christians reflect on the sacrifice of Jesus for the forgiveness of humanity's sins.

Christmas Day

On 25th December, Christians celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ.People go to church for prayers and exchange gifts with their loved ones.Homes are decorated with lights and Christmas trees to celebrate the joyful day.

Easter

Easter Day is celebrated to remember the resurrection of Jesus Christ.It symbolizes hope, new life, and victory over death.People attend church services and enjoy Easter eggs with family and friends.

Hinduism festivals

Durga puja

Durga Puja is a major Hindu festival celebrated primarily in India, especially in West Bengal. It honors the goddess Durga and her victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.

Holi

Holi is the festival of colors. People celebrate with joy, throw colors, and forget old grudges to start anew.

Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the biggest Hindu festivals.it celebrates the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.People decorate their homes with lamps, share sweets, and enjoy fireworks with family and friends.
